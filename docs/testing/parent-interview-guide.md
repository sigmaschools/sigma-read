# Parent Interview Guide — Carolyn Vaughan

**Duration:** 10 minutes max
**Role:** Guide account (parent viewing student data)
**Account:** carolyn@sigmaschool.us / sigma2026

## Setup
Carolyn has 3 students assigned: Emma (active, scores vary), Sofia (active, consistent), and Marcus (struggling).

## Script

### Intro (30 seconds)
"Thanks for looking at this. It's an early version of a reading app Wayne is building. I'm going to show you the guide dashboard — this is what a parent or teacher would see. I just want your honest gut reaction. There are no wrong answers."

### Task 1: First Impression (2 minutes)
Show the guide dashboard. Let her look for 30 seconds, then ask:
1. "What's the first thing you notice?"
2. "Can you tell which student needs the most help?"

### Task 2: Student Detail (3 minutes)
Ask her to click on a student.
3. "What do you learn about this student from this page?"
4. "Is there anything confusing?"

### Task 3: Weekly Summary (2 minutes)
Ask her to click the 📊 Weekly Summary button.
5. "Does this help you understand how your students are doing?"

### Task 4: Trust & Clarity (3 minutes)
6. "If Max were using this every day, would you feel confident you'd know how he's doing?"
7. "What's missing? What would you want to see that isn't here?"
8. "Any last thoughts?"

## Key Signals to Watch For
- Does she immediately understand who's struggling vs. succeeding?
- Does she trust the scores or find them confusing?
- Does she know what to DO with the information? (Action clarity)
- Any confusion about terminology (reading level, score, etc.)
- How long does she spend on the page before speaking? (Cognitive load indicator)

## Notes
- Keep it conversational, not formal
- If she goes on a tangent, let her — parents' natural reactions are signal
- Don't explain what anything means — if she has to ask, that's a finding
- Record exact quotes when possible
