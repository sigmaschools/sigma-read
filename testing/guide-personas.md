# SigmaRead Guide Personas

## 1. Calie Garrett — The Hands-On Guide
**Role:** Lead guide at Sigma School
**Students:** 8 (our test group)
**Tech comfort:** High — uses apps daily, comfortable with dashboards
**Check-in pattern:** Multiple times per day
**What she needs:** Real-time signal on who's engaged and who's struggling. Wants to intervene early, not after the fact.
**Pain point:** Doesn't have time to read every transcript. Needs the dashboard to surface what matters.
**Success:** "I can see at a glance which students need me today."

## 2. Rachel — The Skeptical New Homeschooler
**Role:** Homeschool parent, first year
**Students:** 2 (her own kids, ages 10 and 13)
**Tech comfort:** Medium — uses iPhone, basic apps, not a power user
**Check-in pattern:** Once daily, usually evening
**What she needs:** Confidence that her kids are actually learning, not just clicking through. Evidence she can point to.
**Pain point:** Doesn't trust AI. Worried the reading material might be inaccurate or inappropriate. Wants to see exactly what her kids read and said.
**Success:** "I can see my kids are improving, and I can explain it to my husband."

## 3. Tom — The Overwhelmed Teacher
**Role:** Teacher at a small private school, using SigmaRead as supplemental reading
**Students:** 15
**Tech comfort:** Medium — uses Google Classroom, gradebook software
**Check-in pattern:** Weekly, Friday afternoon
**What she needs:** A summary view. Who improved? Who declined? Who hasn't logged in? She doesn't have time for individual deep-dives on 15 students.
**Pain point:** Information overload. If the dashboard shows her 15 students × 5 sessions each, she'll never get through it.
**Success:** "I can do my weekly SigmaRead review in under 10 minutes."

## 4. David — The Hands-Off Dad
**Role:** Co-parenting, checks in occasionally to feel involved
**Students:** 1 (his daughter, age 12)
**Tech comfort:** High — software engineer
**Check-in pattern:** Sporadic — once a week, maybe less
**What she needs:** A quick "is everything okay?" signal. Green/yellow/red.
**Pain point:** Too much detail is overwhelming. He just wants to know if his daughter is on track.
**Success:** "I opened the app, saw she's doing great, closed it in 30 seconds."

---

## Usage in Testing

Each guide persona tests the GUIDE experience with different goals:
- **Calie:** Full dashboard walkthrough, daily monitoring workflow
- **Rachel:** First-time guide onboarding, reading transcripts for trust
- **Tom:** Scaling test — can the dashboard handle 15 students? Weekly review workflow
- **David:** Minimum viable check-in — what's the fastest path to "everything's fine"?
